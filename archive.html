AI GTM Fieldbook: Google's Ads stack shifts toward agentic marketing workflows

Google's latest Ads and Marketing Live messaging points to a practical shift: ad systems are moving from "set up campaigns and optimize manually" toward more agent-assisted planning, creative, and measurement. For GTM teams, that matters because the bottleneck is no longer just media buying. It is the speed of turning intent data into ads, landing pages, and follow-up journeys that actually match how buyers search and compare. If Google's tools do more of the setup and optimization work, GTM operators will need tighter control over inputs, approvals, and performance review. The winners will be teams that can feed these systems clean offers, strong conversion signals, and crisp guardrails.

AI GTM Fieldbook: AI Search Ads Need A Readiness Audit

Google and OpenAI are moving ads into AI-mediated search and answer surfaces. The immediate GTM question is not whether to move budget. It is whether the team has the inputs needed to test these surfaces without losing control of message, landing page fit, and measurement. OpenAI updated its ad policies on May 26, 2026, adding more detail on placement standards, safeguards, and enforcement. Google announced AI Mode ad formats at Google Marketing Live, including ads that can appear in conversational discovery and recommendation-style answer experiences. Google also said Dynamic Search Ads and related legacy settings will upgrade to AI Max starting in September 2026.
